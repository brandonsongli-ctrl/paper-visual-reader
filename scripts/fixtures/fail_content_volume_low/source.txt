Section 1 (p.1) Introduction. This paper studies the optimal design of information structures
in strategic environments where a sender communicates with a receiver. We develop a general
framework for analyzing Bayesian persuasion problems with multiple receivers and heterogeneous
prior beliefs. Our main contribution is a characterization of the sender's optimal information
policy under general conditions on the receiver's utility function and the state space.

Section 2 (p.4) Model Setup. Consider a finite state space with three states. The sender
chooses a signal structure consisting of a set of signals and a conditional distribution over
signals given each state. The receiver observes the signal realization and updates beliefs
using Bayes rule. The sender's payoff depends on the receiver's action which in turn depends
on posterior beliefs. We assume the receiver's utility is strictly concave in the action.
